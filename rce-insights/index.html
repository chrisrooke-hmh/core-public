Glossary Terminology

  • Users

    The total number of unique users for a given stat.

  • Engaged Sessions

    A session that lasts longer than 10 seconds, has a conversion event, or has at least 2 pageviews or screenviews.

  • Engagement Rate

    The ratio of engaged sessions to total sessions.

  • Average Engagement Time

    The amount of time users spend in engaged sessions.

Screen Size Data Analysis

The data below represents the screen sizes of the devices used to access the RCE.

The screen size is the physical size of the device's display. This is different to the viewport size, which is the size of the visible part of a page within the browser on that device. The size of the screen almost never matches the viewport size, due to the visibility of both operating system and browser interfaces.

This data therefore tells us the sizes of the devices used to access the RCE, but will not always give us a realistic picture of how the content is viewed. For example, a Chrome window that only takes up 2/3 of the screen size will not be captured. Neither will the viewable portion of a browser that is zoomed in for accessibility reasons.

Source embraces responsive design, adapting content and layouts based on the viewer's viewport size and shape. Responsive design extends beyond smaller devices, impacting user experience on larger devices and accommodating accessibility settings. It is important to keep this in mind when considering this data while we do not have viewport data.

Engagement Data Analysis

Our data shows that engagement rates are often as high on mobile devices as desktop, however engagement time is higher on larger devices, this supported by our data.

It is difficult to discern a reason for this directly from the data and more analysis of user flows would be needed. Possibilities include users accessing content on mobile devices for specific discrete tasks; or attemping more general learning and being frustrataed with the mobile experience and leaving for a larger device; or something else.
