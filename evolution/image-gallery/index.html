The Oklahoma Land Rush

The Oklahoma land rush of 1889, was the first of five land runs that drew new settlers to Oklahoma. The land rushes would forever change the shape of tribal lands and pave the way for Oklahoma statehood.

In all, settlers claimed more than 11 million acres of former Indian land in the famous Oklahoma land rush. This huge wave of pioneers was the last chapter of the westward movement and the closing of the “frontier line” between the Mississippi River and the Pacific Coast.
