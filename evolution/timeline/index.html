1820-1830

  • 1803

    Jedediah Smith finds a South Pass through the Rocky Mountains.

    Smith learned about the South Pass from the Crow Indians. It was a much safer and faster trail than the Missouri River route established by Lewis and Clarke in 1804. In time, it became the preferred route for settlers heading west to Oregon and California.
